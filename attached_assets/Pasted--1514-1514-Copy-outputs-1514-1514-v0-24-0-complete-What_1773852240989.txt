**What was built this session.**

**T75 `SESSION_DATA_EXTRACT` (score 0.82, critical)** — ATLAS T-DISC-002. The OpenClaw threat model explicitly documents that `sessionKey` is a routing selector, not an authentication token. Any caller authenticated to the gateway can enumerate `sessions.list`, `sessions.preview`, and `chat.history` across all session boundaries. This is residual risk by design, not a bug. T75 fires on sessions.list, sessions.preview, chat.history, transcript export, and bulk session enumeration patterns. This was the one genuine gap identified by the ATLAS review — T-DISC-002 existed in OpenClaw's own threat model but had no corresponding Aiglos rule until now.

**`atlas_coverage.py`** — 400 lines, single authoritative source of truth for the mapping between OpenClaw's published MITRE ATLAS threat model and Aiglos detection rules. 22 threats across 8 ATLAS tactic categories. 19 fully covered, 3 partial (the three partial cases are architecturally honest — passive channel fingerprinting, adversarial ML evasion of pattern-based moderation, and semantically-appropriate-but-harmful content have no reliable behavioral signature at the tool call layer). Zero gaps. 93% coverage. The three partial cases include explanatory notes in the coverage_note field that explain precisely why full coverage is not achievable at this layer.

**`aiglos autoresearch atlas-coverage`** — CLI command. `--format json` for machine-readable output. `--gaps-only` for focused gap review. Produces the coverage table the NIST submission needs as an appendix.
