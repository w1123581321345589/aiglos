The website needs to do two things simultaneously that most security company sites fail at: make a developer install in 30 seconds, and make a CISO forward a link to their procurement team. Those are two different visitors with two different urgencies, and the site needs to serve both without making either dig.

**The homepage should open with the LiteLLM incident, not a tagline.** Every AI security company leads with "protect your AI" or some variation. Aiglos can lead with proof. "On March 24, a supply chain attack hit a package with 97 million monthly downloads. T81 PTH_FILE_INJECT caught the mechanism before anyone knew it existed. Three of three published OpenClaw GHSAs were caught by rules that existed before the advisories were disclosed." That's not a claim. That's a track record. Below it, the two CTAs side by side: "pip install aiglos" for developers, "See compliance coverage" for the enterprise buyer. Two doors, same product.

**The architecture comparison should be visual and immediate.** The HiddenLayer research makes it clear that every competitor operates at the API gateway or model inference layer. Aiglos operates at the agent runtime layer. A simple diagram showing where each product sits in the stack would be devastating: HiddenLayer sits between the app and the model. Snyk sits at the package registry. OpenShell sits at the container boundary. Aiglos sits inside the agent's execution loop, wrapping every tool call, HTTP request, and subprocess. The visual makes the architectural argument in 3 seconds. No one else occupies that position.

HiddenLayer protects models. Aiglos protects agents. HiddenLayer scans model files before deployment. Aiglos intercepts tool calls during execution. HiddenLayer produces audit-ready logs.
