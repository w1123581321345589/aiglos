**300 KB of test code.** All returning HTTP 200. All actively downloaded by the browser.

The logo situation is my favorite 🖤

The same bear gets downloaded **8 times** per page load:  
3 PNG copies, 2 WebP variants, 2 AVIF variants, and a 512×512 favicon.

Total logo bandwidth: **654 KB** for two logos.

- Image 2: 1.99 MB PNG

The browser explicitly sends:  
`Accept: image/avif, image/webp`

The server ignores it and sends **4 MB of raw PNG** anyway.

With WebP, these would be 200–400 KB combined. That’s **4 MB of pure waste** from just two images.
